What are the common symptoms of iron deficiency anemia?

Iron deficiency anemia is a common condition where the blood lacks adequate healthy red blood cells. Common symptoms include:

  • Fatigue or extreme tiredness: This is the most common symptom.
  • Shortness of breath, especially with exertion.
  • Pale skin, particularly noticeable on the face, nails, and lining of the eyelids.
  • Headaches and dizziness.
  • Cold hands and feet.
Sources:
  • World Health Organization (WHO) Database - Anemia Guidelines
  • National Institutes of Health (NIH) - MedlinePlus
